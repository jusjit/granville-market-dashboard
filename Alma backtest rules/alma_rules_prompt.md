# Alma Stochastic Volatility — Backtested Rule Set (v1.0)
Data: 2025-02-03 to 2026-07-06 | 319 intraday sessions, 66 weekly periods

Apply these rules when interpreting a new Alma post's levels against live price action. Weight VALIDATED rules with full confidence, EMERGING rules as directional hints only, EXPLORATORY rules should not be acted on standalone.

## INTRADAY RULES
- **[VALIDATED]** If SPX opens within 0.3% of the centroid, expect the session's range to touch it. (historical: 92.0%, n=141) — High-confidence baseline. Treat centroid as an active magnet on close-open days.
- **[VALIDATED]** If SPX gaps up more than 0.3% above centroid AND VIX gapped down overnight (vol crush), do not expect a reversion to centroid. (historical: 32.6%, n=43 | out-of-sample: 42.9% (n=14)) — Strongest single actionable filter in the dataset. Do not fade a gap-up on a vol-crush morning expecting mean reversion.
- **[VALIDATED]** If SPX gaps up more than 0.3% above centroid but VIX also gapped up, reversion odds are roughly a coin flip. (historical: 52.7%, n=74) — More tradeable than the vol-crush case but lower conviction. Use as a secondary filter, not standalone.
- **[VALIDATED]** If SPX opens between the downside and upside pivots, expect at least one pivot to be touched during the session. (historical: 86.3%, n=182 | out-of-sample: 79.7% (n=74)) — Pivots function as targets, not containment walls. Softened somewhat out-of-sample (86%->80%) - monitor but still highly actionable.
- **[VALIDATED]** If SPX opens above centroid and inside the pivot range, the upside pivot is favored over the downside pivot. (historical: 70.2%, n=124 | out-of-sample: 67.3% (n=49)) — Use centroid position as the directional tell for which pivot resolves. Held up well out-of-sample.
- **[VALIDATED]** If SPX opens below centroid and inside the pivot range, the downside pivot is favored over the upside pivot. (historical: 60.3%, n=58) — Weaker edge than the above-centroid case (60/47 split vs 70/34) but directionally consistent.
- **[VALIDATED]** Long fly days show the highest centroid magnetism of any identified pattern type. (historical: 81.0%, n=21) — When Alma identifies a long fly, weight the centroid touch expectation upward.
- **[VALIDATED]** IC (iron condor) days show the lowest centroid magnetism of any identified pattern type. (historical: 63.6%, n=33) — When Alma identifies an IC, weight the centroid touch expectation downward relative to baseline (69.7%).
- **[VALIDATED]** The 1-sigma sigma band should be read as an expected target level, not a containment forecast. (historical: 13.0%, n=293) — Do not use the 1-sigma band to size a range-bound trade expecting containment - realized moves exceed it on 87% of sessions. Use as a target reference instead.
- **[VALIDATED]** When the centroid is not touched, the miss is upward (SPX stayed above centroid) far more often than downward. (historical: 78.9%, n=90) — Reflects the sustained uptrend across the sample period. Re-evaluate if the macro regime shifts to sustained downtrend.

## WEEKLY RULES
- **[VALIDATED]** If SPX's weekly open falls between the weekly downside and upside pivots, expect at least one pivot to be touched during the week. (historical: 83.7%, n=43 | out-of-sample: 83.3% (n=18)) — The single strongest weekly signal - barely moved out-of-sample (84.0% -> 83.3%). Lean on this one most heavily.
- **[VALIDATED]** Alma's weekly vol-implied range should be read as a directional map of where volatility is expected to resolve, not a containment forecast. (historical: 7.0%, n=57 | out-of-sample: 0.0% (n=22)) — Upper breaches outnumber lower breaches roughly 1.6-to-1, tracking the broader uptrend. Watch this ratio for macro-regime signal.
- **[EMERGING]** Weekly centroid touch rate. (historical: 62.3%, n=53 | out-of-sample: 47.6% (n=21)) — Dropped meaningfully out-of-sample (71.9% -> 47.6%). Do not treat with the same confidence as the weekly pivot rule yet. Re-test as more weeks accumulate.
- **[EMERGING]** A reversion-model probability of 97% or higher precedes an unusually large 4-week SPX move, but the direction of that move is not predicted by the signal itself. (historical: n/a, n=2) — Both >=97% signals in the dataset (Mar 2025: -10.57% ; Apr 2026: +9.84%) preceded large moves in opposite directions. Use as a 'brace for volatility' flag, not a directional trigger. Needs macro-regime context to disambiguate direction.
- **[EXPLORATORY]** Fly pattern classification (long/short) does not yet reliably predict weekly range behavior. (historical: n/a, n=12) — Sample too small (n=7 long, n=5 short) and both groups show high range utilization similar to unclassified weeks. Do not use standalone.
- **[EXPLORATORY]** Sentiment regime label does not yet show a reliable relationship to weekly SPX return. (historical: n/a, n=13) — Only 13 of 66 weeks tagged. Slowest-building signal since Alma only labels it on regime shifts. Revisit once sample grows past ~30.
